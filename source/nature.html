Pune - The Oxford of the East

-> Attractions Aga Khan Palace Occupying an area of about 19 acre, this palatial mansion was once used as a prison by the British for Mahatma Gandhi, Kasturba Gandhi and Mahadeo Desai, as well as Miraben, Pyarelal Nair, Sarojini Naidu and Dr Sushila Nayar. Bhimashankar Temple Dedicated to Lord Shiva, the Bhimashankar Temple is sixth among the 12 jyotirlingas (devotional shrines of Lord Shiva) in India. Rajasthani and Gujarati influences can be clearly seen in the Nagara (or Indo-Aryan) architectural style of the garbha griha (sanctum sanctorum) and shikahara (tower) of the temple. Dehu The birthplace of Saint Tukaram Maharaj, a Hindu poet, Dehu is around 35 km from Pune. It is located on the banks for River Indrayani. The Dehu Gatha Temple, built in 1723, is a must-visit.
